CBT Questions on Christian Missionaries

Question 1:

According to the lecture, which of the following is NOT one of the mission fields described in Acts 1:8?

  • A. Jerusalem
  • B. Judea
  • C. Bethlehem
  • D. The End of the Earth

Answer: C

Question 2:

What is the primary purpose of missionaries who go to "The End of the Earth"?

  • A. To enjoy different cultures
  • B. To evangelize largely unreached people groups
  • C. To find new places to live
  • D. To escape persecution

Answer: B

Question 3:

Who is considered the Father of Modern Day Missions?

  • A. Paul
  • B. Jim Elliot
  • C. William Carey
  • D. Amy Carmichael

Answer: C

Question 4:

Which missionary continued to preach to the Waorani tribe of Ecuador after her husband was murdered?

  • A. Amy Carmichael
  • B. Elisabeth Elliot
  • C. Lottie Moon
  • D. Adoniram Judson

Answer: B

Question 5:

Which Old Testament figure was sent out by God to the wicked city of Nineveh?

  • A. Abram
  • B. Joseph
  • C. Jonah
  • D. Daniel

Answer: C

Question 6:

Who said, “You can be a goer, you can be a sender, or you can be disobedient. There is no other option but those three”?

  • A. John Piper
  • B. William Carey
  • C. Jim Elliot
  • D. Tyler

Answer: A

Question 7:

According to the lecture, what does "Jerusalem" ministry represent?

  • A. Ministry to those who are located nearby and hold commonalities in culture and socioeconomic status
  • B. Ministry to people who are similar in culture but live far away
  • C. Ministry to those who are very different in culture or socioeconomic status but live nearby
  • D. Ministry to people who live far away and have almost nothing in common

Which Old Testament character was known as a special missionary to Egypt?

  • A. Abram
  • B. Joseph
  • C. Jonah
  • D. Daniel

Question 10:

Which missionary was called by the Lord to go as “a chosen instrument of mine to carry my name before the Gentiles and kings and the children of Israel”?

  • D. Matthew

Question 11:

What was the result of Jonah's mission to Nineveh?

  • B. The people believed God and repented
